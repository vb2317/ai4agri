# Buyer Outreach Targets

Purpose: identify who to show the demo to first, split into two commercial narratives:

1. Procurement Intelligence: forecast supply, center load, acreage confidence, harvest windows, and field verification.
2. Government Intelligence: state-scale crop monitoring, advisories, disaster response, DAC/WebGIS, and Section 12-style tender readiness.

Positioning line:

> We turn satellite, SAR, and field evidence into decision-ready intelligence for agriculture operations.

## Demo To Show

Use the current UPAHAR demo, but change the verbal framing by audience.

For procurement buyers:

- "This predicts where crop supply will come from, when it will arrive, how confident we are, and where field verification is needed."
- Show paddy parcels, temporal imagery, crop confidence, production forecast, PACS/procurement-center load, and Section 12 procurement planning.

For government buyers:

- "This is a Section 12-style agriculture intelligence command system: imagery, crop identification, health, acreage, yield, advisories, procurement, disaster monitoring, WebGIS, mobile, and reports."
- Show reviewer mode, RFP badges, source drawer, coverage matrix, imagery compliance strip, GT flow, and backend/GeoJSON readiness.

## Suggested Outreach Message

Subject: Demo: satellite + field evidence for crop/procurement intelligence

Hi `<Name>`,

I am building a remote-sensing intelligence product that turns satellite/SAR imagery and field evidence into decision-ready outputs: crop class, acreage, confidence, stress, change labels, GT-priority, and procurement/load indicators.

I have a working demo based on a Section 12-style government agriculture RFP. It shows same-area temporal imagery, parcel overlays, crop confidence, field validation, advisory/procurement views, and an audit trail back to the requirement.

I would value 25 minutes to show the demo and test whether this fits your procurement planning / crop monitoring / government reporting workflow.

The goal is not to sell a generic dashboard. It is to identify one narrow pilot where imagery can reduce uncertainty before field teams or procurement teams act.

Best,  
VB

## Demo Call Structure

1. Two-minute context: "This is decision intelligence, not an NDVI dashboard."
2. Show map and temporal imagery.
3. Click parcel: crop, confidence, stress, NDVI/EVI, production estimate.
4. Show procurement or government path depending on buyer.
5. Show GT-needed and validation logic.
6. Close on outputs: dashboard, GeoJSON/API, CSV, report, and pilot package.

## Qualifying Questions

- What operational decision is currently delayed or unreliable?
- Which geography and crop/commodity matters first?
- Do you already have field boundaries or farmer/plot records?
- Do you have field teams or GT data?
- Who consumes the output: procurement, planning, claims, policy, field officers, or executives?
- What output format is most useful: dashboard, API, CSV, GIS layer, PDF report?
- Would a 4-6 week paid pilot be useful if scoped to one AOI and one decision?
